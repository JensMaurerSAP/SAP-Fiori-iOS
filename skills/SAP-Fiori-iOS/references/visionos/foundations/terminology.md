# Terminology — SAP Fiori for visionOS

> SAP Fiori for visionOS | Foundation

## Spatial Computing Terminology

Apple describes visionOS as a "Spatial Computing" platform. The Apple Vision Pro is technically a VR headset with mixed reality (MR) capabilities achieved through external cameras and a LiDAR sensor.

---

## Definitions

### Virtual Reality (VR)
Immerses users in a **completely digital environment**. Headsets block out the real world. Interactions via motion controllers or hand tracking. Common uses: gaming, training simulations, virtual tours.

### Augmented Reality (AR)
Adds **digital content to the real world** without full immersion. Experienced through smartphones, tablets, or AR glasses. Examples: Pokémon GO, navigation overlays.

### Mixed Reality (MR)
**Blends VR and AR** — digital and real-world objects coexist and interact in real-time. Requires advanced hardware (e.g., Microsoft HoloLens). Used in design, education, remote collaboration.

### Extended Reality (XR)
**Umbrella term** encompassing VR, AR, and MR. Reflects the full spectrum of experiences blending physical and digital worlds.

### Spatial Computing
Technologies enabling **digital interactions within physical spaces** — requires understanding of 3D environments. Includes AR, VR, MR, computer vision, and spatial mapping. Emphasizes how users engage with spatial data and surroundings.

---

## Summary

| Term | Scope |
|------|-------|
| VR | Fully digital, real world blocked |
| AR | Digital overlaid on real world |
| MR | Digital + real world interact in real-time |
| XR | Umbrella for VR + AR + MR |
| Spatial Computing | Technologies facilitating all of the above within physical spaces |

visionOS sits at the intersection of MR and spatial computing — primarily MR with full VR capability when needed.
